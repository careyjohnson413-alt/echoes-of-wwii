Stalingrad: The Battle That Broke Hitler's Army

In the winter of 1942, the German 6th Army marched into Stalingrad expecting a swift victory. What followed was the bloodiest battle in human history — a six-month siege of house-to-house combat, starvation, and freezing temperatures that destroyed an entire army and shattered the myth of Nazi invincibility. This is the story of the battle that turned the tide of World War II.
